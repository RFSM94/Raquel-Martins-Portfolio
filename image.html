Client: Faculty of Fine Arts of the University of Lisbon
Role: Web Development, Web Design, Creative Direction, Visual Design

Becoming-image explores how the increasing digitization of images and their immediate online circulation transform our perception of reality. Taking as a starting point a set of one hundred images associated with the most recurrent searches on Wikipedia, the eighth most visited website in the world, this project focuses on the potential of images to shape our collective imaginary. It focuses, in particular, on the availability of images under the Creative Commons license, and their susceptibility to appropriation, modification, sharing and dissemination. The project's intentions and visual exploration was initially displayed through a landing page, taking the shape of a one-page poster with a bold statement.

Through the creation of a navigable three-dimensional space, the project proposes an exploration of iconic images that serve the visual representation of the most searched topics on Wikipedia. This online visual essay promotes immersion in a landscape of cultural references, in complement to a printed publication that contextualizes and expands the topic, as a verbal essay based on the text The Changing Constitution of the Present, by Jacob Lund.

The publication, complimentary to the website's visual essay
Project page and Mobile version

Through these components, Becoming-image reflects on how cultural archetypes are reinforced and shaped by platforms like Wikipedia, highlighting the role of images in mediating and constructing our collective imaginary.
