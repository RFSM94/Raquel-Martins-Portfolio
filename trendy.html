Client: Fine Arts, University of Lisbon
Role: Web Development, Creative Direction, Visual Design

The internet has undergone exponential growth, with posts emerging as its fundamental unit, birthing the ubiquitous feed. But what defines this feed? No longer separate from humanity, the digital realm is intricately woven into our lives. As we are shaped by our consumption, the ramifications of unconscious digital intake loom large. Technology and power are inextricably linked: the former a tool, the latter its culmination. Yet, in a landscape of ever-evolving technology, ignorance breeds vulnerability to subversion.

The first chapter focuses on the meaning of "Posting" in contemporary society

To probe these complexities, one must grasp the interplay between posts and feeds, and confront the ramifications of our passive absorption of information. In (Tr)End(y) Feed, inspired by the insights of Paul Soulellis, my objective is to spotlight the consequential nature of inadvertent content consumption, through a publication which confronts my own feed with scholar's analysis on the topic. Unconscious content consumption not only perpetuates the status quo but also fortifies media and personal agendas. What terrain are we sculpting within our feeds, as they thrive on the content we supply? We feed the post, the post feeds the feed, and the feed, in turn, nourishes us. These questions form the crux of our project, delving into Twitter trends as a tangible case study, exploring how discourses and narratives proliferate within this platform's dynamic feed.

The publication transitions from "The Post" into the Twitter feed problem
The Twitter Feed chapter was designed to be dark, resembling a black box
After gathering the trends from my feed, it was compiled in a Leporello to be side by side with the essay on the topic
This publication gathers both theroy and real samples, to mimic the shape of a smartphone and simulating a feed through the leporello

Despite the platform's opaque selection criteria for trending topics, purportedly based on user profiles and engagement, skepticism abounds. This project juxtaposes my own feed samples against analyses by other scholars, shedding light on the underlying issues. This endeavor aims to disrupt the incessant flow described by Soulellis, prompting deeper reflection on our interaction with digital content. Understanding the feed's societal impact is imperative; unconscious consumption bears consequences, necessitating scrutiny of our digital diet. (Tr)end(y) Feed is an invitation to this reflection.
